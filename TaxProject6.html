Welcome, Trailblazers.

Today, we aren't just doing math; we are advising a growing manufacturing business. Accounting is about helping businesses Discover their financial health.

Professor's Objective By the end of this project, you will master the ability to calculate MACRS depreciation, apply Section 179 limitations strategically, navigate Bonus Depreciation rules, and handle complex lump-sum asset acquisitions.

The Client: Karane Enterprises

Based in College Station, Texas. They began business in 2024. As they scale up, they are acquiring significant assets. Your job is to maximize their tax efficiency.

Key Constraints:

  • Calendar-year taxpayer.
  • They want to maximize deductions (Section 179).
  • We will ignore Bonus depreciation until Module 3.

Module 1: The Foundation (2024 Assets)

In 2024, Karane acquired three key assets. Our goal is to compute the maximum depreciation deduction, utilizing Section 179.

Asset Cost Date Placed in Service Recovery Period
Office Furniture $250,000 Feb 3 (Q1) 7 Years
Machinery $1,660,000 July 22 (Q3) 7 Years
Used Delivery Truck $60,000 Aug 17 (Q3) 5 Years
Strategy Tip: Section 179 Application To maximize deductions, apply the Section 179 expense to assets with the longest recovery periods or lowest first-year percentage first. Why? Because the remaining basis gets depreciated. You want the "slowest" assets to be expensed immediately!
CHECKPOINT Which asset gets §179 Priority?

We have a §1,220,000 limit available (Example calculation). Should we apply it to the Truck (5-year) or the Machinery (7-year)?

Correct Answer: The Machinery.

The Machinery has a 7-year life. The Truck has a 5-year life. By applying §179 to the Machinery first, we write off the asset that would otherwise take longer to depreciate. This accelerates the tax benefit.

Module 2: Expansion (2025 Assets)

The company grew in 2025. Now we have new assets and new complexities. We must assume the 2024 assets are now in their second year of MACRS.

2025 Acquisitions

  • Computers ($420k): 5-year property.
  • Luxury Auto ($85k): 5-year property (Subject to IRS Tables).
  • Assembly Equipment ($1.2M): 7-year property.
  • Storage Building ($800k): 39-year Non-residential Real Property.
Crucial Concept: Mid-Quarter vs. Half-Year Always check when assets were placed in service. If >40% of tangible personal property is placed in service in Q4, you MUST use the Mid-Quarter convention.

Check the dates: Computers (Mar), Auto (May), Equipment (Aug). Nothing in Q4. We use Half-Year Convention.
CHALLENGE Luxury Auto Limits

The auto cost $85,000. Can we depreciate all of that using regular MACRS percentages?

No. Luxury automobiles are subject to strict annual caps (Listed Property rules). Regardless of the 200% DB calculation, you are capped at the IRS limit table for the specific year placed in service.

Module 3: Advanced Strategy (Bonus Depreciation)

In Part C, Karane changes their mind. They want to use Bonus Depreciation.

Bonus Depreciation allows an additional percentage of the asset's cost to be deducted immediately after §179 but before regular MACRS.

The Order of Operations
  1. Section 179: Take the specific dollar amount off the top.
  2. Bonus Depreciation: Apply the % (e.g., 40%, 60%, 80% depending on tax year) to the remaining basis.
  3. Regular MACRS: Depreciate whatever is left using the standard table rates.

Note for this project: Pay close attention to the specific Bonus percentages provided in the problem set (e.g., 40% vs 0%). This dramatically alters the "MACRS Basis" column.

Module 4: The Acquisition

Karane buys a competitor for a lump sum of $1,469,800. This is not just one asset; it is a basket of assets.

Allocation Strategy

You must allocate the purchase price to the specific assets (Inventory, Furniture, Machinery, Patent, Building, Land). Whatever is left over is Goodwill.

Asset Type Treatment
Inventory Cost of Goods Sold (Not depreciated)
Goodwill & Patents Section 197 Intangibles. Amortized over 15 years (180 months), straight-line, full-month convention.
Land Never depreciated.
CALCULATION Amortizing Goodwill

If Goodwill is $3,800 and purchased in September, how do we calculate the Year 1 deduction?

Formula: (Cost / 180 months) × Months owned.

Acquired Sept 15. Count September as a full month (Full-month convention for intangibles).

Sept, Oct, Nov, Dec = 4 Months.

Calculation: ($3,800 / 180) × 4 = $84 (rounded).

Module 5: Compliance (Form 4562)

The final step is translating our calculations to the IRS Form 4562.

Section Breakdown

  • Part I: Section 179 Election. (This is where your $1,200,000 for Assembly Equipment goes).
  • Part II: Special Depreciation (Bonus).
  • Part III, Section B: Current year assets (2025) not listed elsewhere.
  • Part IV: Summary (The Grand Total).
  • Part V: Listed Property (The Luxury Auto goes here, NOT in Part III).
Common Pitfall Do not double count! If you put the Luxury Auto in Part V, do not enter it again in Part III. The total from Part V flows to Part IV, Line 21.
